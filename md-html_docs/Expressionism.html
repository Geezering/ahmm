Expressionism: Key Facts and Context

Main Locations

  • Germany (especially Dresden, Berlin, Munich), with influence spreading to Austria and other parts of Europe

Relevant Previous Art Movements

  • Symbolism: Focus on inner feelings and imagination
  • Post-Impressionism (especially Van Gogh and Munch – used colour and brushwork to show emotion)
  • Art Nouveau and Neo-Impressionism: Influenced some Expressionists

Culture and Politics

  • Emerged during a time of rapid urbanisation, social change, and anxiety about the modern world (pre-, during, and post-World War I)
  • Artists responded to alienation and chaos, often expressing pessimism, frustration, and a search for spiritual renewal

Contemporary Inventions and Technology

  • Growth of cities, electric lighting, photography, and early cinema influenced new themes and visual styles
  • Printmaking, particularly woodcuts, was popular for its bold, graphic qualities

Fashion of the Day

  • Men: Suits with high collars, waistcoats, and hats; artists sometimes chose looser, practical clothes for the studio
  • Women: Long skirts and blouses (early 1900s), later shorter skirts and simpler, more practical dress after World War I

Artists and Period

  • Period: 1905–1925

Ernst Ludwig Kirchner

Ernst Ludwig Kirchner, Street, Berlin 1913
  • Pioneered jagged lines, distorted shapes, and unnatural colours to express emotion and inner turmoil
  • Painted urban scenes full of tension, anxiety, and chaos
  • Co-founded Die Brücke (“The Bridge”), striving for a new and expressive art
  1. Used jagged lines and bright, unnatural colours for emotion
  2. Painted city scenes full of tension and anxiety
  3. Helped lead the Die Brücke group for new, expressive art

Emil Nolde

Emil Nolde, Prophet 1912
  • Used vivid, clashing colours and rough brushwork for spiritual and emotional intensity
  • Created mystical, dreamlike scenes and powerfully expressive landscapes and religious imagery
  • Distorted and exaggerated forms to capture feeling, often making scenes appear dreamlike or nightmarish
  1. Used vivid colours and rough brushwork for spiritual intensity
  2. Painted mystical, dreamlike scenes
  3. Expressed strong feelings through distortion and exaggeration

Käthe Kollwitz

Käthe Kollwitz, Die Mutter 1922–23
  • Focused on suffering, social injustice, and the struggles of ordinary people through prints and sculpture
  • Used bold forms and dramatic contrasts of light and dark for emotional power
  • Created art with empathy and social criticism, aiming to inspire compassion and change
  1. Focused on suffering and social injustice
  2. Used bold forms and contrasts for emotional power
  3. Created prints and sculptures to inspire empathy and social change

Key Themes, Ideas, and Artistic Aspects

    • Expressionism is about showing feelings over realistic appearance
    • Artists distorted shapes, used bold or dark colours, and painted with rough brushwork to express powerful emotions
    • Their art often appears dramatic, unsettling, or intense—reflecting the anxiety and intensity of early 20th-century life
    • Expressionists wanted viewers to feel something strong, not just admire technical skill

Expressionism Was:

  • A movement where artists put their feelings and ideas at the centre, using dramatic techniques to make viewers sense the emotions and turmoil of modern life
